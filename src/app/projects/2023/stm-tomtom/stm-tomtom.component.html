Project Overview

In our LOG430 STM project, we developed a resilient architecture ensuring high availability and performance. We optimized for hardware failures and balanced resource use for sustained performance, focusing on continuous service.

Project Impact

The impact of the project's first iteration was profound, particularly in handling computation failures. Chaos Monkey tests showed that artifacts like STM-1, TripComparator, and RouteTimeProvider were vulnerable to crashes. However, since TripComparator and RouteTimeProvider are stateless, the node controller efficiently restarted them upon failure. For STM-1, a more complex approach was required. When STM-1 crashed, a heartbeat detected the fault, triggering active redundancy. This process switched operations to STM-2, which seamlessly fetched data from STMDB1 where STM-1 left off, ensuring uninterrupted service continuity and robust system performance.

In the second iteration of our project, we faced additional challenges with database and RabbitMQ crashes. To address these, our performance strategy involved three key tactics. First, we increased the resources of the STM databases (STMDB1 and STMDB2) to handle larger workloads and ensure data availability. Secondly, we maintained multiple copies of computation with STM-1 and STM-2, providing a fail-safe in case one system failed. Lastly, we reduced overhead between STM and TripComparator by utilizing RabbitMQ, which streamlined the communication and processing, enhancing overall system efficiency and reliability.
